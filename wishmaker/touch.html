It’s been bugging me for a while now. I’m not quite sure why. Maybe it’s the desire for something more that inundates my thoughts after countless hours of boring college assignments and the dim glow of a computer screen. Whatever it is, I’m merely a subject to it—a thought so enthralling that I know I should disregard it for my own good: What if we could do whatever we want? What if we were limitless?

The roof of my shitty two-story house is awash with dew, and my body lies aching. My limbs are glued to the tile from hours of immobility. The camera is perched high above my head, aimed directly above, recording all it sees. I hope today’s footage is the one that breaks the mold its predecessors have failed to crack. Countless hours of digital memory have been erased with nothing to show. If technology could shriek, mine would be piercing.

They’re finally beginning to fade. I trace the shapes of each constellation as the morning sun begins to peek over the trees and chase them into space, hoping that I haven’t missed a single wayward anomaly. The prospect of the glow is tantalizing, and I wish there were more signs other than splotches of light when I blink. My body loves to trick me, much to my overly ambitious college-student-level chagrin.

If my thoughts were a journal, would anyone care to read them? I feel as if my existence is tethered to this roof, forever searching the stars for a dream that might as well not exist. But it could, couldn’t it? Maybe that’s why I keep going. It’s a nice thought.
